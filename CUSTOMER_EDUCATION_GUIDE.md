## Purpose

Govern **customer education** surfaces: Learn hub, articles, contextual explainers, and help-adjacent teaching moments.

Education increases competence. It is not a blog for SEO hype and not a substitute for ledger truth.

## North star (`EP-029`)

> What should I understand before I act?

## Principles

### EDU-001: Glossary-aligned terms

Use `GLOSSARY.md` and `FINANCIAL_MICROCOPY_GUIDE.md`. Never invent parallel names for accrued, credited, available, withdrawable, pending, reservation.

### EDU-002: Short beats clever

Prefer one-screen lessons: problem → definition → what you’ll see in the app → next step. Avoid longform essays on money home.

### EDU-003: Contextual first

Best education is linked from empty states, status help, and withdrawal expectancy—not a mandatory course wall.

### EDU-004: Honest uncertainty

When timing depends on review or banking rails, say so. Do not invent SLA theater.

### EDU-005: No performance promises

Education never implies guaranteed ROI, risk-free investing, or regulatory credentials the platform does not hold.

### EDU-006: Teaching ≠ interception

Do not block deposit or withdrawal behind “read this article.” Optional “Learn why” links only.

### EDU-007: Journey staging

Organize Learn by journey:

1. Getting started / verification  
2. Funding your wallet  
3. Choosing and understanding investments  
4. Accrued vs credited & NY settlement  
5. Withdrawals & review  
6. Referrals  
7. Statements & records  
8. Security basics  

### EDU-008: Visual calm

Diagrams OK if they clarify (e.g. accrued vs credited). No celebratory gamification of “modules completed” that pressures funding.

### EDU-009: Maintenance

Education content is product-owned. Policy changes that affect customer language require content update in the same release train as the behavior change.

### EDU-010: Accessibility

Articles must be keyboard readable, heading-structured, with alt text for diagrams and readable contrast.

| Evergreen concepts | FAQs + troubleshooting + contact |
